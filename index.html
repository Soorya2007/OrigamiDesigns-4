Interesting Facts About Chameleons

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are a part of the Iguana Suborder
  • Changing colors is an important part of communication among Chameleons
  • Most Chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Interesting Facts About Pandas

  • Giant pandas (often referred to as simply “pandas”) are black and white bears.
  • These magnificent mammals are omnivores.
  • The giant panda’s scientific name is Ailuropoda melanoleuca, which means ‘black and white cat-foot‘.

Interesting Facts About Camels

  • There are mainly two species of true camels: the Dromedary and the Bactrian.
  • Camels can survive in harsh environments. They can survive without food and water for long
  • Camels can drink nearly 200 litres of water in one day. Camels have two lips which they can move independently to graze and eat leaves of thorny desert bushes without hurting themselves.

Interesting Facts About Cicadas

  • Cicadas are part of a subspecies called Magicicada
  • "Cicada" comes from the Latin, meaning "tree cricket."
  • While cicadas are often colloquially referred to as a kind of locust, they are not part of the locust family.

Interesting Facts About Pigeons

  • Pigeons Have Been Present In Human History For Thousands Of Years
  • Pigeons Can Be Extremely Colorful Living Relatives Of The Dodo
  • They Are Capable Of Superstitious Behavior

Interesting Facts About Teddy Bears

  • German toy maker Margarete Steiff made a stuffed bear after a trip to the zoo sparked her nephew to design a stuffed bear he asked her to make in 1903
  • After the Titanic sank in 1912, German toy company Steiff created 500 teddy bears to honor the victims
  • The stuffing was unhygienic until husband-and-wife toymakers Wendy Boston and Ken Williams invented washable teddy bears in 1954
